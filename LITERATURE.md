# Literature Review: Image/Video Representations + RWKV + JEPA/MAE for Time-Series Forecasting (ETTh1)

**Scope.** Synthesis of 6 subtopic surveys aimed at one concrete problem: our VideoRWKV / VideoRWKVJEPA model renders time series into image/video frames and forecasts with an RWKV backbone (+ a co-trained MAE/JEPA aux loss), but **underfits a 1-matrix linear baseline** on ETTh1 h96 (our video model **0.586** vs **NLinear 0.389**). The literature is read for *why* this happens and *what is falsifiably worth trying* to close that gap.

---

## 1. Executive summary

The literature is consistent and unusually decisive for our case: image/video representations of time series **can** match or beat native sequence models on ETT-scale forecasting, but **only one recipe reliably does so** — VisionTS's *periodicity-keyed 2D reshape into a per-channel grayscale image, instance-normalized, with masked reconstruction reusing a strong pretrained MAE* (ETTh1 h96 MSE ≈ 0.390, i.e. matching NLinear's 0.389). Line plots (ViTST) and GAF/MTF/recurrence plots win only for **classification** or when **fused**, and are lossy single-views for forecasting; spectrograms can beat raw rasters but the lesson is that the *image transform itself is the lever*, not "use a vision model." Our underfitting is **over-determined by documented anti-patterns we hit simultaneously**: (1) we overlay all 7 channels into one small frame — the VisionTS++ "multivariate gap" and the worst-case indiscriminate per-timestep channel mixing that the channel-independence literature (PatchTST, DLinear, Han et al.) shows overfits/underperforms on ETT; (2) we render a frame-as-token, destroying the intra/inter-period spatial locality that makes vision backbones useful (VisionTS) and quantizing amplitude into a bounded pixel grid (ViTime's formal quantization error bound) that a linear map never pays; (3) we **co-train** an MAE/JEPA aux loss against the forecast loss, which no canonical masked-modeling paper does (all use pretrain→finetune with a *separate, usually linear* decoder, encoder-sees-only-visible, high/structured mask ratios), and for JEPA specifically an EMA target + anti-collapse term is mandatory or the representation collapses — exactly the underfitting symptom. Every SOTA linear-recurrence TS model (RWKV-TS, S-Mamba, TimeMachine, BlackGoose Rimer) keeps **per-channel temporal patch tokens** and never renders to a frame, and the dominant ETT signal is the **linear lookback→horizon map** (DLinear/SpaceTime), so the fact that we lose to a 1-matrix forecaster points squarely at the *input representation and a missing linear path*, not RWKV capacity. The highest-leverage, cheapest, most falsifiable moves are therefore: add a linear/NLinear residual path (guarantees ≤ ~0.389), go channel-independent + periodicity-aligned + instance-normed rendering with continuous (un-rasterized) patches, and stop the aux loss from competing (forecast-only control, then high-mask pretrain→finetune, or a horizon-conditioned JEPA with EMA+VICReg) — each with a concrete MSE inequality against the 0.389 bar.

### 2.1 Encoding time series AS images for forecasting (GAF / MTF / recurrence plots / line plots / periodicity-reshape) + vision backbones

Our underfit is over-determined by overlaying 7 channels (VisionTS++ gap), frame-as-token (loses period locality), and pixel rasterization (ViTime quantization error a linear map never pays).

| HiMTM: Hierarchical Multi-Scale Masked TS Modeling with Self-Distillation | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.05012 | Hierarchical multi-scale masking + cross-scale self-distillation (teacher-student) for long horizons; multi-scale targets + distillation/EMA stabilize pretraining vs single-scale pixel recon. |

**Takeaway:** masked reconstruction DOES help forecasting, but only under a recipe our model violates: pretrain-then-finetune with a separate (usually linear) decoder discarded for forecasting; encoder sees only visible tokens; HIGH/structured mask ratios (75% images, 90–95% redundant video, frame/series not per-point); and for latent (JEPA) an EMA target + anti-collapse term. We break the first three and possibly the fourth. Highest-value: the cheap forecast-only (aux=0) control, then high-mask pretrain→finetune; VisionTS suggests warm-starting from a strong pretrained MAE.

### 2.3 JEPA family (I-JEPA / V-JEPA / V-JEPA 2) adapted to TS forecasting

| Title | Year | Link | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-Supervised Learning from Images with a JEPA (I-JEPA) | 2023 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.08243 | Predict latent reps of large masked target blocks from a context block. Context encoder + predictor by SGD; target encoder = EMA copy w/ stop-grad; targets LayerNorm-normalized; L2/smooth-L1 latent loss. | Joint Embeddings Go Temporal (TS-JEPA) | 2025 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25449 | Most direct TS adaptation. ~10 patches, mask 70%, EMA m=0.998, smooth-L1 latent loss. **Caution:** in their own short-term forecasting Table 2 the plain AR/MAE baseline often BEATS JEPA (e.g. ETT AR 0.009 vs TS-JEPA 0.017). | HEPA: Self-Supervised Horizon-Conditioned Event Predictive Architecture for TS | 2026 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11130 | Causal Transformer pretrained with a JEPA objective where a HORIZON-CONDITIONED predictor forecasts FUTURE representations (not values); freeze encoder, finetune only the predictor. Reportedly beats PatchTST/iTransformer/MAE/Chronos-2 on ≥10/14 benchmarks, ~10× fewer tuned params. The predictive (forecasting-shaped) JEPA target transfers, not masked-infill. *(2026 preprint — verify before citing.)* |
| Connecting JEPA with Contrastive SSL (C-JEPA) | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19560 | I-JEPA's EMA alone is insufficient against collapse; predictor poorly learns the mean of patch reps. Adds VICReg variance+covariance regularization → faster convergence, better downstream. A drop-in anti-collapse/anti-underfit fix. |
| LeJEPA: Provable and Scalable SSL Without the Heuristics | 2025 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08544 | Balestriero & LeCun: optimal JEPA embedding distribution is isotropic Gaussian, enforced via Sketched Isotropic Gaussian Regularization (SIGReg). NO stop-grad, NO EMA/teacher-student, NO schedulers, single hyperparameter, linear cost. Candidate to replace a brittle EMA/stop-grad stack. |
| VisionTS (as above) | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.17253 | Forecasting = period-aligned 2D image reconstruction (inpaint masked future) with a frozen ImageNet MAE; #1 zero-shot on GIFT-EVAL. **Takeaway:** JEPA's core kit is small and established (context enc + EMA/stop-grad target + predictor + L1 latent loss + copy-breaking masking; collapse prevented by EMA + target norm, optionally VICReg/SIGReg). But for FORECASTING MSE, latent-prediction JEPA tends to LOSE to plain reconstruction/AR (TS-JEPA's own Table 2; VisionTS wins with MAE recon). The two adaptations that transfer are HEPA's horizon-conditioned future-rep prediction and VisionTS's period-aligned image + reconstruction head. Priority: demote JEPA to an aux regularizer with a period-aligned recon primary; if keeping JEPA, switch to horizon-conditioned + add VICReg/diagnose collapse; audit EMA/target-norm for a stale teacher.

### 2.4 Channel independence vs channel mixing in multivariate TSF — implications for image/MAE forecasters

| Title | Year | Link | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Are Transformers Effective for TS Forecasting? (DLinear/NLinear) | 2022/AAAI-23 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.13504 | A single-layer linear map (DLinear = decomp + linear; NLinear = subtract-last + linear) beats all then-SOTA Transformers on 9 LTSF benchmarks. The bar to beat on ETT is a ~1-matrix channel-independent linear forecaster — exactly what we fail. |
| A Time Series is Worth 64 Words (PatchTST) | 2022/ICLR-23 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.14730 | Patching + channel-independence (shared univariate backbone, no cross-channel mixing) win; MAE-style masked pretraining on per-channel patch tokens transfers. | The Capacity and Robustness Trade-off: Revisiting Channel Independence (Han et al.) | 2023/TKDE-24 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.05206 | Channel-dependent (CD) = higher capacity but overfits + fragile under drift; channel-independent (CI) trades capacity for robustness, generalizes better on small data (ETT). | iTransformer: Inverted Transformers Are Effective for TS Forecasting | 2023/ICLR-24 Spotlight | https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.06625 | Each whole-variate series = one token; attention ACROSS variates, FFN over time. | CARD: Channel Aligned Robust Blend Transformer | 2023/ICLR-24 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.12095 | Pure CI throws away channel correlations; re-adds via channel-aligned attention + token-blend + a horizon/uncertainty-weighted robust loss to stop CD overfitting. Cross-channel needs an explicit robustness mechanism on ETT. |
| From Similarity to Superiority: Channel Clustering (CCM) | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01340 | Per-pair benefit of mixing correlates with intrinsic channel similarity. Cluster similar channels, share weights within cluster (CI across, CD within); +2.4% long-term avg. Mix only similar channels, not all 7 indiscriminately. |
| SOFTS: Efficient MTSF with Series-Core Fusion (STAR) | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.14197 | Linear-complexity centralized mixer: aggregate all series into one global "core", redistribute back per channel — instead of all-pairs attention. Robust under drift / many channels; cheap drop-in for a per-channel RWKV. |
| VisionTS (as above) | 2024 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.17253 | Renders ONE univariate channel to grayscale, MAE-inpaints the masked future. Explicitly CHANNEL-INDEPENDENT ("multivariates are intractable to encode in a single image"); folds RGB to gray. Our all-7-channels-in-one-frame is the documented anti-pattern. |
| VisionTS++ (as above) | 2025 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.04379 | Names our exact gap ("multivariate-forecasting gap between fixed RGB-3-channel vision models and arbitrary-variate series"); fixes via colorized multivariate conversion (each variate its own subfigure/color). SOTA GIFT-Eval, 6–44% MSE reduction. |
| TSMixer: An All-MLP Architecture for TS Forecasting (Google) | 2023 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.06053 | Alternating time-mix / channel-mix MLP. On ETT-scale only matches CI linear/PatchTST; cross-variate mixing pays off mainly on large M5 retail. Channel-mixing benefit grows with data scale, marginal on ETT. |

**Takeaway:** the ETT bar (NLinear 0.389) is a CI ~1-matrix linear forecaster; CI trades capacity for robustness and wins on small/drifting ETT. Mixing helps only at the right granularity (variate-tokens/iTransformer, or a centralized core/SOFTS — not per-timestep/per-pixel), gated to similar channels (CCM), and explicitly regularized (CARD/PRReg) — and even then the ETT gain is small. The SOTA image/MAE forecaster (VisionTS) is itself strictly CI; our shared 7-channel frame forces worst-case indiscriminate per-timestep mixing. Fix: render per-channel + go CI, verify the render is information-preserving via a linear probe, and add cross-channel back only as a single late similarity-gated regularized variate-token fusion.

**Takeaway:** every SOTA linear-recurrence TS model keeps per-channel temporal patch tokens and adds channel mixing as an explicit learnable module — none render channels to an image. RWKV capacity is almost certainly NOT the bottleneck.

### 2.6 Why image/video/2D reps underperform simple linear/patch models on LTSF, and the documented remedies

| Title | Year | Link | Finding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Are Transformers Effective for TS Forecasting? (DLinear/NLinear, LTSF-Linear) | 2022/2023 (AAAI-23 Oral) | https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.13504 | A single linear layer beats all then-SOTA Transformers on ETT/Weather/Electricity; NLinear ETTh1 h96 ~0.39. The bar to clear; a trend-capturing linear path carries performance. |
| VisionTS (as above) | 2024 (ICML 2025) | https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.17253 | The ONE image-as-TS method that is competitive (ETTh1 h96 ~0.390, ETTm1 ~0.374) — works via 3 non-vision tricks: (1) periodicity-aligned reshape P × ⌊L/P⌋, (2) RevIN-style instance norm to fixed std r=0.4, (3) strict channel independence (3 duplicated grayscale channels; color didn't help). | Reversible Instance Normalization (RevIN) | 2022 (ICLR) | https://openreview.net/forum?id=cGDAkQo1C0p | Symmetric per-instance normalize-then-denormalize with learnable affine removes non-stationary mean/var shift; model-agnostic, large long-horizon gains. | Harnessing Vision Models for TS Analysis: A Survey | 2025 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.08869 | Frames design axes ("how to encode as images", "how to model the imaged series"); flags continuous-series vs discrete-pixel discrepancy and pre/post-processing (normalization, de-imaging) as where info is lost. **Takeaway:** image/2D/video reps lose to a 1-matrix linear forecaster on ETT for concrete reasons: rendering quantizes amplitude into a bounded fixed-resolution canvas (info bottleneck + scale-insensitivity), fusing variates into one small frame forces overfit-prone mixing into too few pixels, and spatial/locality inductive bias mismatches phase-structured temporal data. The one competitive image method (VisionTS ≈ 0.39) wins from TS-specific fixes — periodicity-aligned reshape, RevIN, strict channel independence — not vision priors. Highest-leverage: (1) sum in a linear/NLinear residual, (2) make the render periodicity-aligned + instance-normed, (3) render channel-independently, (4) verify a norm-only/linear-only baseline and down-weight the recon aux.

## 4. What to try first

**Top idea = #1 (linear/NLinear residual path), then #4 (forecast-only control) as the decisive cheap diagnostic.** These are the lowest-effort, highest-confidence moves and they are nearly free to run together. Concretely in our `VideoRWKV` / `VideoRWKVJEPA`:

- Add a default-OFF flag, e.g. `--video_linear_residual` (with `--video_residual_alpha` for the `α` blend), that wires a per-channel `NLinear` (subtract-last + single `Linear(seq_len → horizon)`) skip **summed** with the existing RWKV+frame head, the whole thing RevIN-wrapped. Setting `α=0` recovers a pure linear path (must reproduce ~0.389) so the change is byte-identical-at-default and additive, matching the repo's additive/default-OFF convention.
- Run the **aux=0 control** with the existing aux-weight flag set to 0 in the same sweep cell, GPU-gated, so we immediately learn whether the co-trained MAE/JEPA loss is interference (Hypothesis B: forecast-only `< 0.586` and `< 0.389`) before investing in idea #5/#6/#8.

Pass/fail bar for both: **denorm MSE ≤ 0.389 (NLinear) on ETTh1 h96, and strictly below the current 0.586**, 3 seeds. If #1 clears 0.389 it proves the underfit was a missing-linear-path + lossy-render problem (not RWKV capacity); if the aux=0 control already beats co-training, drop or down-weight the SSL term and proceed to per-channel + periodicity-aligned rendering (#2, #3).

---

### 3-sentence summary of top recommendations

1. The single highest-leverage fix is to **sum a per-channel linear/NLinear residual path into the RWKV+frame output (RevIN-wrapped)**, which guarantees the model cannot lose to NLinear by much (test: ETTh1 h96 denorm MSE ≤ 0.389 and < the current 0.586, with `α=0` reproducing ~0.39).
2. In parallel, run the **forecast-only (aux-weight = 0) control** — the cheapest decisive test of whether the co-trained MAE/JEPA loss is pure interference — and only keep SSL if it provably beats this control, otherwise move to high-mask pretrain→finetune or a horizon-conditioned JEPA with EMA+VICReg.
3. Then make the rendering match the only competitive image-of-TS recipe (VisionTS): **channel-independent (one frame per variate, batch B·V), periodicity-aligned reshape (P≈24), instance-normalized, with continuous un-rasterized float patches** — each step independently falsifiable against the 0.389 bar with its own negative control (mis-period regression, bit-depth monotonicity, per-channel vs shared-frame).
